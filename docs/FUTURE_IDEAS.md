# Future Ideas

This file is for ideas that are interesting but not part of the current milestone.

The purpose of this file is to preserve excitement without derailing progress.

## Product Areas

Forge may eventually be organized around three primary areas:

### Today

A practical daily view for acting in the moment.

Possible features:

* Start a workout
* See recent workout history
* See current lift cues
* See previous notes for planned movements
* Show suggested focus for the day
* Quick checkboxes for habits
* Show “Today’s Proof”

### Movements

A history and coaching view for each major lift or movement.

Possible features:

* Movement detail pages
* Exercise history
* Recent sets, reps, and weights
* Active coaching notes
* Technique cues
* Progression rules
* Personal records
* Volume history
* Pain/recovery notes connected to a movement

### Review

A trend and reflection view.

Possible features:

* Weekly training review
* Monthly training review
* Workouts per week
* Habit consistency
* Lift progress over time
* Identity wins
* Recovery and pain patterns
* Visual dashboard cards and charts

## Dashboard Ideas

Forge may eventually include visual dashboards to show progress over time.

Potential charts:

* Back squat working weight over time
* Bench working weight over time
* Bent over row working weight over time
* RDL working weight over time
* Sumo deadlift working weight over time
* Workouts per week
* Volume by movement
* Volume by week
* Exercise frequency
* Time since last trained movement
* Post-meal walks per week
* Protein-first meals per week
* No-zero-day actions per week
* Identity wins over time
* Pain notes by body area
* Pain/recovery notes by movement

Dashboard guiding questions:

* What should I do today?
* Am I getting stronger?
* Am I becoming more consistent?
* What habits are slipping?
* What movements need attention?
* What evidence have I collected that I am becoming the person I intend to be?

## AI Features

AI is not part of Version 1.

Possible future AI features:

* Paste messy workout notes and have AI parse them into structured data
* Generate weekly training reviews
* Recommend the next workout based on recent history
* Flag possible overuse, pain, or recovery issues
* Summarize trends across months of workouts
* Generate “coach notes” after each workout
* Convert ChatGPT fitness advice into movement-specific coaching notes
* Identify current cues for each movement
* Notice patterns between habits, recovery, pain, and workout performance

## Coaching Notes

Forge may eventually store durable coaching notes connected to movements.

Examples:

* Back Squat: Traps into bar
* Back Squat: When you think you are at the bottom, keep going
* Back Squat: Own 205 before moving up
* Bench Press: Hold 145 until all three sets of eight are clean
* Overhead Work: Watch neck and shoulder stiffness two days later

Coaching notes should be manually editable.

Some coaching notes may be marked as active/current so they appear in the Today view.

## Habit / Identity Tracking

Forge may eventually track daily identity evidence alongside workout data.

Potential habits:

* Walk after breakfast
* Walk after lunch
* Walk after dinner
* Protein first at meals
* Daily movement
* No-zero-day action
* Recovery choice
* Did the planned workout
* Did a smaller version instead of skipping

The goal is not shame-based habit tracking.

The goal is to collect evidence of becoming the kind of person who consistently does the things he values.

Possible language:

* Today’s Proof
* Identity Wins
* Evidence Collected
* Non-Negotiable Actions
* Forge Streak
* Proof of Becoming

## Pain / Recovery Tracking

Forge may eventually track pain and recovery notes as training context.

Possible features:

* Pain notes by body area
* Pain notes by movement
* Recovery notes
* Energy notes
* Sleep notes
* Flags for recurring patterns
* Searchable history for phrases like “neck stiffness,” “low back,” or “plantar fascia”

This should not become medical diagnosis.

It should help with better training decisions.

## Commercial Possibility

Forge is being built for personal use first.

If it becomes genuinely useful, possible future audiences might include:

* Busy dads rebuilding health
* Former athletes returning to consistency
* People who want identity-based habit support
* People who want a training log with coaching memory

Commercial ideas should not drive Version 1.
